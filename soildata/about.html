About

Soil fertility is crucial for agriculture, supporting the 60% of the global population that relies on farming for survival. However, many soils are nutrient-deficient or contaminated, complicating crop growth and necessitating better soil assessment methods. Traditional trial-and-error approaches are inefficient and harmful to the soil, prompting the need for smart monitoring sensors. These sensors collect and analyze soil data, guiding farmers in selecting suitable crops and preserving soil health. Additionally, integrating plants that naturally enrich the soil promotes sustainable agriculture, enhancing food security while benefiting the environment.
